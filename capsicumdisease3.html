VERTICILLIUM WILT

: Verticillium wilt is caused by the soil-borne fungi Verticillium dahliae and Verticillium albo-atrum. It causes yellowing, wilting, and drying of leaves. Infected stems may show brown discoloration in the vascular tissues.

Symptoms:

  • Yellowing and wilting of leaves, starting from the lower leaves and progressing upwards.
  • Browning of vascular tissue in the stem when cut open.
  • Leaf chlorosis and necrosis, with patches of pale green or yellow discoloration turning brown or necrotic.
  • Stunted growth, reduced vigor, and diminished fruit production.

Precautions:

  • Soil sanitation
  • Irrigation management
  • Soil health management
  • Avoid excessive nitrogen

Recommendations:

  • rotating with non-host crops can help reduce their population.
  • practice soil solarization by covering the soil with transparent plastic sheets during hot summer months.
  • Maintain good soil health and fertility to enhance the capsicum plants' vigor and ability to withstand disease pressure.
  • Consult with local agricultural authorities or plant pathologists for approved fungicides and proper application methods.

Organic Control:

  • Crop Rotation:

    capsicum crops with non-host plants for at least three years to reduce the population of Verticillium fungi in the soil.

  • Soil Amendments:

    soil health and increase its natural resistance by incorporating organic matter, such as compost or well-rotted manure, into the soil.

  • Resistant Varieties:

    Select capsicum varieties that are known to have resistance or tolerance to Verticillium wilt. Consult with local agricultural experts or seed suppliers for recommended resistant varieties.

  • Biological Control:

    Apply beneficial soil microbes, such as Trichoderma spp. or Bacillus spp., to the soil to help suppress the growth of Verticillium fungi.
