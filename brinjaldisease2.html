VERTICILLIUM WILT

Verticillium wilt is a destructive fungal disease that affects brinjal (eggplant) plants, caused by the soilborne fungus Verticillium dahliae. The disease initially causes wilting and yellowing of the lower leaves, which gradually spreads upward through the plant. The wilting is often more pronounced during hot weather. Verticillium wilt is characterized by the presence of dark streaks in the vascular tissues of the infected plant, which can be observed by cutting through the stem. Symptoms:

  • Yellowing and wilting of leaves, starting from the lower leaves and progressing upwards.
  • Browning of vascular tissue in the stem when cut open.
  • Leaf chlorosis and necrosis, with patches of pale green or yellow discoloration turning brown or necrotic.
  • Stunted growth, reduced vigor, and diminished fruit production.

Precautions:

  • Soil sanitation
  • Irrigation management
  • Soil health management
  • Avoid excessive nitrogen

Recommendations:

  • rotating with non-host crops can help reduce their population.
  • practice soil solarization by covering the soil with transparent plastic sheets during hot summer months.
  • Maintain good soil health and fertility to enhance the brinjal plants' vigor and ability to withstand disease pressure.
  • Consult with local agricultural authorities or plant pathologists for approved fungicides and proper application methods.

  • Resistant Varieties:

  • Biological Control:

    Apply beneficial soil microbes, such as Trichoderma spp. or Bacillus spp., to the soil to help suppress the growth of Verticillium fungi.
